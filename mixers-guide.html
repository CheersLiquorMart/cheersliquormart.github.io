Cocktail Mixers Guide

The essential components that transform spirits into delicious cocktails

RETAIL SELLING TIP

When a customer purchases a spirit, always suggest a high-quality mixer to go with it: "This premium gin would pair perfectly with our Fever-Tree tonic—it won't mask the delicate botanicals like regular tonic water would."

What Are Mixers?

Definition: Mixers are non-alcoholic liquids used to dilute, balance, and enhance the flavors of spirits. They form the backbone of cocktail creation, turning strong alcohols into palatable, complex drinks.

Selling Points

  • Essential Role: They temper the strong bite of alcohol and introduce complementary notes of sweetness, acidity, or effervescence.
  • Variety: From sodas and tonics to fresh juices and syrups, mixers offer a range of options—from basic to gourmet—that can elevate any cocktail.
  • Completion Factor: Remind customers that a good mixer is the difference between a harsh drink and a balanced, enjoyable cocktail experience.

Types of Mixers

Sodas & Sparkling Waters

Examples: Tonic water (with natural sugar), club soda, ginger ale

Sales Angle: Essential for highball cocktails and classics like gin and tonics; quality mixers (e.g., Fever-Tree) enhance taste and refreshment.

Key Cocktails: Gin & tonic, vodka soda, whiskey ginger, Moscow mule

Fresh Juices

Examples: Lemon, lime, grapefruit; seasonal options like blood orange or pomegranate

Sales Angle: Fresh-squeezed juices cut through alcohol's intensity and add brightness—ideal for sour-type cocktails.

Key Cocktails: Margarita, whiskey sour, paloma, daiquiri

Syrups

Examples: Simple syrup (1:1 sugar to water), honey syrup, maple syrup, spiced syrups

Sales Angle: Offer controlled sweetness and consistency. Homemade syrups allow customers to avoid preservatives and tailor flavor to their cocktails.

Key Cocktails: Old fashioned, mint julep, French 75, mojito

Specialty Mixers

Examples: Bittersweet mixers, shrub (vinegar-based syrups), or house-made mixers featuring seasonal ingredients

Sales Angle: These add layers of complexity and seasonal flair, making standard cocktails unique and modern.

Key Cocktails: Craft variations of classic drinks, "signature cocktails" for home entertaining

Best Practices for Mixers

  • Invest in Quality: Encourage customers to opt for mixers with natural ingredients. Explain that cheap, artificial mixers can ruin a cocktail's balance.
  • Fresh Is Best: Emphasize the benefits of fresh-squeezed juices or home-made syrups for brighter, more natural flavors.
  • Balance Is Key: Explain that a well-balanced cocktail should touch all five taste points—sweet, sour, bitter, salty, and sometimes umami. Mixers are critical to achieving this balance.
  • Seasonal Rotation: Suggest rotating mixers based on the season—spiced syrups in the fall, fresh fruit juices in the summer—to keep cocktails current and appealing.

DIY Syrup Recipes to Share with Customers

Basic Simple Syrup: Equal parts sugar and water, brought to a simmer until sugar dissolves. Cool and store in refrigerator for up to one month.

Honey Syrup: 3 parts honey to 1 part hot water, stir until combined. Perfect for whiskey cocktails.

Spiced Syrup: Add cinnamon sticks, star anise, or cloves to simple syrup while simmering. Great for fall and winter drinks.

Herb-Infused Syrup: Add fresh herbs (mint, rosemary, basil) to hot simple syrup and steep for 30 minutes before straining. Excellent for gin or vodka cocktails.

Practical Tips for Selling Mixers

  • Highlight DIY Benefits: Offer simple recipes for homemade syrups and fresh juices. Providing recipe cards can drive sales of sugar, citrus, or even specialty mixers.
  • Cross-Sell with Spirits: Bundle mixers with complementary spirits (e.g., a gin and tonic package featuring premium gin and Fever-Tree tonic) to encourage complete cocktail solutions.
  • Educate on Pre-Batching: Teach customers that pre-batching mixers for parties saves time and ensures consistent flavor, making them ideal for entertaining.
  • Display Quality Brands: Stock reputable brands and point out the differences—such as natural sugar tonics versus artificial ones—so customers know they're getting the best.
  • Emphasize Value Proposition: While premium mixers cost more than generic options, they're still affordable compared to the spirits themselves and make a noticeable difference in the final drink.

Spirit and Mixer Pairings to Suggest

  • Gin: Premium tonic water, cucumber water, elderflower syrup
  • Vodka: Cranberry juice, grapefruit juice, ginger beer
  • Whiskey: Ginger ale, apple cider, quality cola, cherry syrup
  • Rum: Pineapple juice, coconut water, lime juice, mint syrup
  • Tequila: Fresh lime juice, grapefruit soda, agave syrup, jalapeño syrup
