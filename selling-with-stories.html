Selling with Stories: A Guide for Cheers Liquor Mart Staff

Learn how storytelling can enhance customer interactions and boost sales.

Why Stories Really Matter

  • Stories hook customers and make them curious
  • Products with stories seem more valuable and special
  • Customers will pay more for drinks with interesting backgrounds
  • Stories turn regular shopping into an experience
  • Customers who hear stories remember your store and come back

Ready-to-Use Product Stories

BOURBON: Jefferson's Ocean

The Simple Story: This bourbon travels on ships across the ocean. The waves, changing temperatures, and salty air give it unique flavors.

What to Say: "This bourbon actually ages while sailing at sea! The rocking of the ship and ocean air make it taste different from regular bourbon. People love how each batch is a little different depending on the voyage. It costs more because of this special aging process - but customers say it's worth every penny."

BOURBON: E.H. Taylor Tornado

The Simple Story: A tornado hit the storage building where this bourbon was aging. Instead of ruining it, the exposure to the weather made it taste amazing.

What to Say: "See this bourbon? It survived a real tornado! The barrels were exposed to the elements, and it created a one-of-a-kind flavor. It's rare because it can't be made again - nature created something special by accident. That's why collectors get excited about it."

WINE: The Prisoner

The Simple Story: This wine breaks all the rules. It's bold and different, just like its name and the artwork on the bottle.

What to Say: "Looking for something different? This wine is called The Prisoner because it doesn't follow the usual wine rules. The winemaker blends different grapes that aren't usually mixed together. Even wine experts were surprised by how good it tastes! The cool label is based on a famous painting, which makes it stand out on any dinner table."

TEQUILA: Código 1530

The Simple Story: This tequila is made the old-fashioned way in Mexico, using clay ovens and wooden tanks like they did hundreds of years ago.

What to Say: "This tequila is made just like they did it in old Mexico. For years, it was a secret tequila only shared among Mexican families and not sold in stores. They still cook it slowly in clay ovens. That's why it tastes smoother than ordinary tequila - it's made with extra care and time."

VODKA: Crystal Head

The Simple Story: This vodka comes in a cool skull bottle designed by a famous actor. It's filtered through real crystals to make it extra smooth.

What to Say: "Check out this skull bottle! It was created by actor Dan Aykroyd who wanted to make the purest vodka possible. The vodka inside is filtered through actual crystals, making it super clean-tasting. People often keep the empty bottle as decoration afterward - it's like getting two products in one!"

How Stories Boost Sales

SELLING TIP

When a customer hesitates about a higher-priced item, a good story can justify the price. Focus on what makes the product special and worth the extra cost.

  1. Stories Justify Higher Prices
    • When customers know why something costs more, they're more willing to pay
    • "This costs a bit extra because of the special aging process at sea"
    • "You're not just buying the drink - you're buying the story to share at your party"
  2. Stories Create Emotional Connection
    • Customers buy with emotions first, then justify with facts
    • "Imagine serving this to friends and telling them about the tornado that created it"
    • "This makes a great gift because there's a cool story to tell when they open it"
  3. Stories Make You Sound Knowledgeable
    • Customers trust staff who know product backgrounds
    • You don't need to be an expert - just share these simple stories confidently
    • Being able to tell stories makes you different from cashiers at other stores
  4. Stories Make Shopping Fun
    • Customers enjoy learning something new while shopping
    • They'll remember our store as the place that makes buying liquor interesting
    • They'll tell friends about both the product AND the shopping experience

Tips for Using Stories Every Day

  1. Start with your best story for new customers
    • Hook them right away with something interesting
    • Watch their reaction to see if they enjoy stories
  2. Keep a few stories ready for different types of customers
    • Budget shoppers: Stories about good value
    • Gift buyers: Stories about impressive bottles
    • Party planners: Stories about crowd-pleasing drinks
  3. Practice telling stories to coworkers
    • The more you tell them, the more natural they sound
    • Ask which parts of the story they found most interesting

Remember: You don't need to be a great storyteller! Just share these simple facts with enthusiasm. Even a 30-second story can turn a regular sale into a memorable experience that brings customers back.
