Beer Styles Guide

From lagers to stouts, learn the characteristics of major beer styles to better serve your customers.

Why Beer Styles Matter

Understanding beer styles helps you guide customers to beers they'll enjoy based on their taste preferences. This guide covers the major beer families and their most popular styles, with key characteristics to help you make knowledgeable recommendations.

QUICK SELLING TIP

When a customer is unfamiliar with craft beer, ask about other beverages they enjoy. Wine drinkers might appreciate Belgian styles, cocktail fans often enjoy IPAs, and coffee lovers frequently enjoy stouts and porters.

Lagers

Lagers are crisp, clean beers fermented with bottom-fermenting yeast at cold temperatures. They're often lighter in flavor and color than ales.

Customer Recommendation Guide

Use these suggestions to help recommend beers based on customer preferences:

FOR WINE DRINKERS

Red Wine Lovers: Belgian Dubbel, Flanders Red Ale, Brown Ale, Porter

White Wine Lovers: Belgian Witbier, Hefeweizen, Berliner Weisse, Saison

FOR SPIRITS DRINKERS

Whiskey Fans: Barrel-aged Stouts, Scotch Ale, Old Ale

Vodka/Gin Drinkers: Pilsner, Kölsch, American Wheat, Session IPA

FOR BEGINNERS

Gateway Craft Beers: Wheat Ales, Amber Ales, Blonde Ales, Fruit-infused styles

Seasonal Recommendations

  • Spring: Maibock, Saison, Kölsch, Pale Ale
  • Summer: Wheat Beers, Session IPA, Pilsner, Gose
  • Fall: Märzen/Oktoberfest, Brown Ale, Pumpkin Ale, Amber Ale
  • Winter: Stouts, Porters, Winter Warmer, Belgian Strong Ales, Barleywine
