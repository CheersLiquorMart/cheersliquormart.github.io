Local Brewery Highlights

Learn about our local breweries to help customers support local businesses and find their new favorite craft beers.

Why Local Matters

Supporting local breweries benefits our community in several ways. Local breweries create jobs, contribute to tourism, often use environmentally sustainable practices, and help develop unique regional flavors and brewing traditions. Many customers specifically seek out local options to support their community.

QUICK SELLING TIP

When customers are browsing the craft beer section, ask if they've tried any of our local breweries' offerings. Mention that "drinking local" supports our community's economy and provides fresher beer, since it doesn't travel as far from production to consumption.

Featured Breweries

These local breweries have established themselves as cornerstones of our craft beer scene. Learn about their stories, specialties, and flagship beers to better guide your customers.

Est. 1994 5.2 miles away

Bristol Brewing Company

Family-owned brewery located in the historic Ivywild School, specializing in consistent, quality ales and lagers with a strong community focus.

Family-Owned Taproom Food Available Dog-Friendly
Flagship Beers We Carry:
  • Laughing Lab Scottish Ale - Award-winning amber ale with caramel notes
  • Beehive Honey Wheat - Light, crisp wheat beer with local honey
  • Mass Transit Ale - Amber ale with balanced hops and malt
  • Compass IPA - West Coast-style IPA with pine and citrus notes

Laughing Lab is their most popular beer and has won multiple GABF medals. Great for customers who find IPAs too bitter.

Est. 2013 4.8 miles away

Red Leg Brewing Company

Military-themed brewery founded by a veteran, with a spacious new facility featuring a beer garden and food hall.

Veteran-Owned Taproom Food Hall Outdoor Seating
Flagship Beers We Carry:
  • Blue Nose Brown - Smooth brown ale with chocolate and toffee notes
  • Howitzer Amber - Balanced amber ale with caramel sweetness
  • Devil Dog Stout - Dry Irish-style stout with coffee and chocolate flavors
  • Cutter Wit - Refreshing Belgian-style witbier with orange and coriander

Mention the military connection for service members or veterans looking for local beer options.

Est. 2016 3.9 miles away

Cerberus Brewing Company

Gastropub-style brewery in a converted auto shop, known for creative beer styles and exceptional food offerings.

Gastropub Full Restaurant Patio Seating Rotating Taps
Flagship Beers We Carry:
  • Elysium IPA - Balanced IPA with tropical fruit notes
  • NBD Kolsch - Clean, crisp German-style ale
  • Tiny Umbrella New England IPA - Hazy IPA with low bitterness, high juicy hop flavor
  • The Viktor Pale Ale - Citrus-forward American pale ale

Great suggestion for customers who enjoy dining out - mention they can visit the brewery for excellent food paired with these beers.

Est. 2008 6.3 miles away

Trinity Brewing

Artisanal brewery specializing in farmhouse ales, sour beers, and barrel-aged creations with an environmentally conscious approach.

Artisanal Sour Specialists Eco-Friendly Vegetarian Options
Flagship Beers We Carry:
  • Elektrick Cukumbahh - Cucumber saison, refreshing farmhouse ale
  • 7 Day Sour - Approachable, kettle-soured beer with fruit variations
  • Red Swingline IPA - Citrusy IPA with brettanomyces character
  • Magical Brettanomyces Tour - Farmhouse series showcasing wild yeast

Recommend to experienced craft beer fans looking for something unique or to wine drinkers who might appreciate the complexity of Trinity's sours.

Est. 1993 7.1 miles away

Phantom Canyon Brewing Co.

Downtown brewpub in a historic building, one of the city's original craft breweries offering diverse beer styles and full restaurant service.

Historic Building Full Restaurant Pool Tables Multi-Level
Flagship Beers We Carry:
  • Alpenglow Amber - Malty amber ale with caramel notes
  • Queen's Blonde Ale - Crisp, approachable blonde ale
  • Streamliner IPA - Modern American IPA with citrus hop profile
  • Two Step Hefeweizen - Traditional German wheat beer

A good recommendation for visitors or newcomers to the area due to its historical significance and downtown location.

Est. 2011 17.2 miles away

Pikes Peak Brewing Co.

Monument-based brewery named after the famous mountain, producing clean, well-crafted beers inspired by local geography.

Mountain-Inspired Taproom Live Music Food Trucks
Flagship Beers We Carry:
  • Devil's Head Red - Malty red ale with caramel and light hop bitterness
  • Gold Rush Belgian - Belgian-style golden ale with fruit and spice notes
  • Summit House IPA - Bold, pine-forward IPA with a clean finish
  • Elephant Rock IPA - Juicy New England-style IPA with tropical notes

Their mountain-named beers are great for visitors looking to bring home a taste of Colorado's outdoor culture.
