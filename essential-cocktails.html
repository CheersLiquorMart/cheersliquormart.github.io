Essential Cocktails Guide

Popular cocktail recipes to help customers gather the right ingredients

RETAIL SELLING TIP

When a customer is buying a specific spirit, suggest complementary ingredients for popular cocktails. For example, if they're buying gin, ask if they have vermouth and bitters for a martini, or tonic water and lime for a gin and tonic.

Classic Cocktails

Old Fashioned

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz bourbon or rye whiskey
  • 1 sugar cube or ¼ oz simple syrup
  • 2-3 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Orange peel for garnish

Glassware: Rocks glass

Selling Tip: Suggest premium bourbons like Woodford Reserve or Buffalo Trace for a more refined flavor. Recommend Luxardo cherries as a premium garnish option.

Martini

Ingredients:

  • 2.5 oz gin or vodka
  • 0.5 oz dry vermouth
  • Lemon twist or olives for garnish

Glassware: Martini glass or coupe

Selling Tip: For gin martinis, recommend botanical-forward gins like Hendrick's or Tanqueray No. Ten. For vodka martinis, suggest premium options like Grey Goose or Belvedere.

Manhattan

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz rye whiskey or bourbon
  • 1 oz sweet vermouth
  • 2-3 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Maraschino cherry for garnish

Glassware: Coupe or martini glass

Selling Tip: Recommend Rittenhouse Rye or Bulleit Rye for an authentic Manhattan. Suggest Carpano Antica Formula vermouth for a premium experience.

Popular Contemporary Cocktails

Moscow Mule

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz vodka
  • 4-6 oz ginger beer
  • ½ oz lime juice
  • Lime wedge for garnish

Glassware: Copper mug

Selling Tip: Suggest premium ginger beers like Fever-Tree or Q Ginger Beer. Recommend copper mugs as an upsell for the authentic experience.

Margarita

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz tequila (blanco or reposado)
  • 1 oz orange liqueur (Cointreau or Triple Sec)
  • ¾ oz fresh lime juice
  • Salt for rim (optional)
  • Lime wheel for garnish

Glassware: Margarita glass or rocks glass

Selling Tip: Recommend 100% agave tequilas like Patrón or Casamigos. Suggest Cointreau as a premium orange liqueur option over generic triple sec.

Negroni

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz gin
  • 1 oz Campari
  • 1 oz sweet vermouth
  • Orange peel for garnish

Glassware: Rocks glass

Selling Tip: Suggest Tanqueray or Bombay Sapphire gin. Recommend Carpano Antica Formula or Cocchi Vermouth di Torino for the sweet vermouth component.

Refreshing Summer Cocktails

Mojito

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz white rum
  • ¾ oz fresh lime juice
  • ½ oz simple syrup
  • 6-8 mint leaves
  • Club soda
  • Mint sprig for garnish

Glassware: Highball glass

Selling Tip: Recommend Bacardi Superior or Havana Club 3 Year for an authentic Cuban flavor. Suggest a muddler as an additional purchase for proper preparation.

Paloma

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz tequila (blanco)
  • ½ oz lime juice
  • Grapefruit soda (like Squirt or Jarritos)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Grapefruit wedge for garnish

Glassware: Highball glass

Selling Tip: Suggest Espolòn or Olmeca Altos tequila. Recommend Jarritos grapefruit soda for an authentic Mexican flavor.

Aperol Spritz

Ingredients:

  • 3 oz prosecco
  • 2 oz Aperol
  • 1 oz club soda
  • Orange slice for garnish

Glassware: Wine glass

Selling Tip: Recommend La Marca or Mionetto prosecco. Suggest Aperol as an essential aperitif for any home bar, especially for summer entertaining.

After-Dinner Cocktails

Espresso Martini

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 oz vodka
  • 1 oz coffee liqueur (Kahlúa)
  • 1 oz freshly brewed espresso
  • ½ oz simple syrup
  • Coffee beans for garnish

Glassware: Martini glass

Selling Tip: Suggest premium vodkas like Ketel One or Grey Goose. Recommend Mr Black Coffee Liqueur as a premium alternative to Kahlúa.

White Russian

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz vodka
  • 1 oz coffee liqueur (Kahlúa)
  • 1 oz heavy cream

Glassware: Rocks glass

Selling Tip: Recommend Stolichnaya or Absolut vodka. Suggest Bailey's Irish Cream as an alternative to heavy cream for a richer flavor.

Selling Strategies for Cocktail Ingredients

  • Bundle Recommendations: When a customer purchases a base spirit, suggest the additional ingredients needed for popular cocktails. For example, if they buy tequila, recommend lime juice, Cointreau, and salt for margaritas.
  • Quality Matters: Emphasize that using fresh ingredients and premium spirits significantly improves the cocktail experience. Fresh lime juice tastes much better than bottled, and premium spirits create a smoother cocktail.
  • Proper Tools: Suggest essential bar tools like shakers, jiggers, and muddlers to help customers create professional-quality cocktails at home.
  • Glassware Pairings: Recommend appropriate glassware for specific cocktails, explaining how the right glass enhances both presentation and drinking experience.
  • Seasonal Suggestions: Adjust your cocktail recommendations based on the season—refreshing drinks like Mojitos and Aperol Spritz in summer, warming cocktails like Old Fashioneds and Manhattans in winter.
