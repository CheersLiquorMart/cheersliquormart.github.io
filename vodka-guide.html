Vodka Guide

Understand the world's most versatile spirit to provide expert recommendations to your customers

What is Vodka?

Vodka is a spirit stripped down to its basics—neutral and smooth. It's made to be nearly flavorless, making it the perfect canvas for cocktails. Ingredients typically include grains (wheat, rye, corn) or potatoes.

Key Features of Vodka

  • Neutrality: The goal is a clean, unobtrusive taste.
  • Multiple Distillations: Often distilled several times for purity.
  • Versatility: Ideal for mixing in a wide range of cocktails or serving chilled in shots.

Types of Vodka & Their Best Uses

Standard (Unflavored) Vodka

  • Prioritizes purity and smoothness
  • Goes through multiple distillations
  • Examples: Absolut, Tito's, Grey Goose

Best for: Mixing in any cocktail from a classic Vodka Tonic to a Moscow Mule. Its clean, neutral profile lets other ingredients do the talking.

Flavored Vodka

  • Infused with flavors like citrus, berries, or spices
  • Perfect for fruity or dessert cocktails
  • Examples: Absolut Citron, Smirnoff Vanilla

Best for: Cocktails where you want an extra twist of flavor without the need for additional ingredients—think a Cosmopolitan with a kick or a fruity spritz.

Premium Vodka

  • Uses higher-quality ingredients or unique bases (like grapes or organic wheat)
  • Often marketed as extra-smooth with subtle nuances
  • Examples: Beluga, Crystal Head, Chopin (potato-based)

Best for: Martinis and other cocktails where the vodka is a prominent component. The smooth texture and subtle flavors shine in simple recipes.

Rye Vodka

  • Made from rye grains
  • Examples: Chopin Rye

Best for: Cocktails that benefit from a spicier, robust character. It can add a subtle heat and earthiness to a martini or a Bloody Mary.

Potato Vodka

  • Made from potatoes instead of grains
  • Examples: Chopin Potato, Luksusowa

Best for: Neat sipping or cocktails where a creamier, fuller texture is desired. Its smooth finish works well in simple mixes.

Craft/Artisanal Vodka

  • Small-batch production methods
  • Often emphasizes local ingredients or unique production techniques

Best for: Unique, small-batch cocktails where you want a local or innovative edge. These often have a distinct character based on the ingredients and process used.

The Vodka-Making Process

  1. Fermentation: Convert starches from grains or potatoes into alcohol.
  2. Distillation: Multiple rounds of distillation remove impurities and flavor.
  3. Filtration: Commonly filtered through charcoal, quartz, or even other media to enhance clarity.
  4. Dilution & Bottling: Dilute to about 40% ABV (80 proof) and then bottle.

How to Serve Vodka

  • Classic Cocktails: Think Moscow Mule, Bloody Mary, and Cosmopolitan.
  • Chilled Shots: In some traditions (especially Eastern European), vodka is best served ice-cold.
  • On the Rocks: Premium vodkas can be enjoyed over ice with a twist of citrus.

QUICK SELLING TIP

When a customer is unsure about vodka selections, focus your recommendations on how they plan to enjoy it. For mixed drinks, suggest mid-tier options that offer good value. For martinis or neat sipping, guide them toward premium options where smoothness makes a noticeable difference.
